Phi Sigma Rho Sorority Officer

Phi Sigma Rho is a social sorority for women in engineering and STEM majors that aims to provide academic excellence with a social balance. There are over 35 chapters nationwide.

I started my journey with Phi Sigma Rho as a founding member of the Alpha Mu Chapter at University of Colorado, Boulder. Together with eight other women of varying engineering majors, I learned about the history, traditions, and values of Phi Sigma Rho. During this time, I served as colony secretary in charge of weekly colony communications and meeting notes. I aided in writing our chapter bylaws and tracking various other requirements for installation. Our hard work paid off and we were installed as a new chapter in January of 2017.

After installation, I was elected into the position of Vice President of Standards, which I held for two years. My duties as VP of Standards included maintaining and editing the chapter bylaws, tracking and aiding active members in meeting their active point requirements (participation in various sorority events and functions), and leading the Standards Board, a committee of four members and myself in charge of enforcing chapter bylaws and taking disciplinary action when necessary. During my time as VP of Standards, I helped write and pass over five major pieces of legislation that were added to the chapter bylaws, and made numerous smaller edits and clarifications to the chapter bylaws. As such, I am confident in my ability to write clearly and concisely.
