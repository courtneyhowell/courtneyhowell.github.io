Micron Internship

May 2018 - present

I have worked as a SSD Hardware Intern full time for two summers and part time during my senior year of university. My projects and major responsiblilties during each time period are explained in greater detail below.

Summer 2019

I had three major projects over the course of the summer.

The first project was to build and test a proposed solution to a backup power issue seen with great prevalence on one of the drive programs. My responsibility was to build three added circuits on a proto-board and test drive operation with the added circuits. In my testing, I had to ensure that the new hardware did not interfere with normal drive operation, while still fixing the issue during the case of a bakcup capacitor short.

The second project was to perform characterization testing on a new revision of drives and compile the results into cohesive and concise reports. Tests that I was responsible for included basic drive functionality, backup capacitor short test and backup hardware verification, input voltage margining, brownout testing, power rail sequencing for both startup and turnoff conditions, power consumption characterization, and voltage rail noise under load testing. These tests were performed on 3 different form factors of NVMe SSD.

The third project was designing, guiding layout, and release to fab of a SSD-to-host-computer adapter board. The adapter board design was based on an older board from a previous SSD program, so using this reference board as a base, I investigated the functionality of each component and circuit, eliminated any circuits whose functions were never used or no longer needed, and added a couple circuits that will be needed for the upcoming drive program. Design, schematic capture, BOM generation, and (upcoming) layout of the adapter board were all done using Cadence.

In addition to the main projects, I helped team members with miscellaneous testing tasks, including soldering test point wires or coax cables, replacing surface mount parts, taking waveforms and measurements on the oscilloscope, and loading programmable voltage regulator chip firmware.

2018-2019 School Year

During the academic year, I interned part time. My responsibilities during this time consisted primarily of completing testing and release of the adapter board that was designed and laid out toward the end of summer and performing and documenting various SSD characterization tests. Tests that I performed included basic drive power-on, brownout testing, power rail sequencing for both startup and turnoff conditions, power consumption characterization, system clock jitter characterization, and voltage rail noise under load testing.
